## Ultrasound Report — Renal

### Clinical Indication

Type 2 diabetes mellitus with recent lab findings of elevated creatinine (1.1 mg/dL), reduced eGFR (62 mL/min/1.73m²), and microalbuminuria (ACR 47.4 mg/g). Evaluate for diabetic nephropathy and renal structural changes.

### Technique

Real-time B-mode and Doppler ultrasound evaluation of both kidneys and urinary bladder performed in supine and lateral decubitus positions.

### Comparison

No prior renal ultrasound available for comparison.

### Findings

**Right Kidney:** Measures 10.2 x 4.8 x 4.5 cm. Normal position and orientation. The renal parenchyma shows mildly increased echogenicity (Grade I) compared to the adjacent liver parenchyma, suggestive of early parenchymal disease. Cortical thickness measures approximately 1.3 cm (lower limit of normal). No focal lesions identified. No hydronephrosis or calculi. Normal corticomedullary differentiation is partially maintained.

**Left Kidney:** Measures 10.0 x 4.6 x 4.3 cm. Similar findings to the right kidney with mildly increased parenchymal echogenicity (Grade I). Cortical thickness approximately 1.2 cm. A simple cortical cyst measuring 1.2 x 1.0 cm is noted at the upper pole, meeting all criteria for a Bosniak category I cyst. No hydronephrosis, calculi, or solid masses.

**Renal Doppler:** Bilateral renal arteries demonstrate normal waveform morphology. Resistive index (RI) measures 0.72 on the right and 0.74 on the left (upper limit of normal 0.70). No evidence of renal artery stenosis. Mildly elevated RI bilaterally may reflect early intrarenal vascular changes consistent with the clinical history of hypertension and diabetes.

**Urinary Bladder:** Adequately distended. Normal wall thickness. No intraluminal lesions or calculi. Post-void residual volume: 25 mL (normal).

### Impression

1. Bilateral mildly increased renal parenchymal echogenicity (Grade I), consistent with early medical renal disease. In the clinical context of diabetes and hypertension with documented microalbuminuria and reduced eGFR, findings are suggestive of early diabetic nephropathy.
2. Mildly elevated resistive indices bilaterally (0.72–0.74), which may reflect early renovascular changes secondary to hypertension and/or diabetes.
3. Simple left upper pole renal cyst (Bosniak I) — no follow-up required.
4. Normal kidney sizes bilaterally. No hydronephrosis, calculi, or obstructive uropathy.

### Recommendation

Clinical and laboratory correlation recommended. Consider nephrology referral for comprehensive diabetic kidney disease management. Follow-up renal ultrasound in 12 months if clinically indicated.
